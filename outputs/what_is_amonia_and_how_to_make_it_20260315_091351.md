## 🔑 Key Concepts

* **Definition:** Ammonia is a colorless, highly toxic gas with a characteristic pungent odor, composed of nitrogen and hydrogen atoms.
* **Chemical Formula:** NH3
* **Molecular Weight:** 17.03 g/mol
* **Critical Temperature:** -27.9°C
* **Critical Pressure:** 112.8 bar
* **Key Properties:** Highly soluble in water, highly toxic, pungent odor

## 📚 Detailed Explanation

### History of Ammonia

Ammonia was first discovered by Joseph Black in 1756, but the first industrial production method was developed by Justus von Liebig in 1815. However, it was Carl Bosch and Fritz Haber who revolutionized the industrial production of ammonia through the Haber-Bosch process in 1909. This process is still used today to produce approximately 140 million tons of ammonia annually.

### Production Methods

There are three primary methods of producing ammonia:

* **Haber-Bosch Process:** The most widely used method, which involves the reaction of nitrogen and hydrogen gases in the presence of a catalyst.
* **Birkeland-Eyde Process:** An older method that involves the electrolysis of nitrogen in the presence of a catalyst.
* **Direct Nitrogen Fixation (DNF) Process:** A newer method that involves the use of a microorganism to convert atmospheric nitrogen into ammonia.

### Applications

Ammonia has numerous applications in various industries, including:

* **Fertilizer Industry:** Ammonia is the primary raw material for urea production, which is essential for agriculture.
* **Pharmaceutical Industry:** Ammonia is used as an intermediate in the production of several pharmaceuticals, such as captopril and enalapril.
* **Food Industry:** Ammonia is used as a food additive, preservative, and flavoring agent.
* **Dyes Industry:** Ammonia is used as a solvent and intermediate in the production of dyes.

### Safety Precautions

Ammonia is highly toxic, corrosive, and flammable. Prolonged exposure to ammonia can cause irritation to eyes, skin, and respiratory system. It is essential to handle ammonia with caution and follow proper safety protocols.
